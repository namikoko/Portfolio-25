01

Executive Summary

Redesigning a powerful but complex integration for clarity at scale.

Everflow's Google Ads integration was powerful but difficult to navigate at scale. Users had to manage campaigns, ad groups, and ads across dense tables and inconsistent patterns. This complexity slowed down high-value workflows for affiliate managers and media buyers.

I redesigned the experience to reduce cognitive load, strengthen the visual hierarchy, and create a predictable structure that supports accounts of any size. The result was a system that feels faster, cleaner, and easier to use for both new and expert users.

→
Faster navigation across large accounts
↓
Lower support burden for ad management questions
↑
Improved adoption of the integration after launch
⟳
New system patterns used by engineering for future expansions

03

The Strategic Problem

Uncovering the deeper issues behind surface-level complexity.

The Google Ads integration exposed a deeper issue inside Everflow. The platform was powerful but scattered. Users had to make sense of large datasets inside an environment that was not designed to scale.

01
Campaigns, ad groups, and ads shared similar layouts but behaved differently
02
Tables were dense and required too much scanning
03
Key actions were buried inside inconsistent side panels
04
Filters were placed in different locations on each page
05
Users lost time switching between views during routine work
06
Large accounts felt heavy and difficult to navigate

The problem was not appearance. The problem was lack of structure, predictability, and clarity.

The issue was fragmentation and cognitive overload across multiple workflows.

04

Research Insights

What users struggled with and what they actually needed.

I interviewed affiliate managers, traffic teams, and advertisers to understand how they worked inside the integration.

✕

What users found difficult

Dense information that slowed decision-making
Similar screens that did not follow the same rules
Filters that behaved differently across pages
Side panels that were not predictable
Complex rule building with too many text blocks
Difficulty scanning large lists quickly
✓

What users needed

Clear hierarchy
Predictable patterns
Faster ways to scan and sort
Fewer clicks for frequent actions
A cleaner model for campaign structure
A simplified system for editing and reviewing data

Research confirmed the need for a unified system rather than isolated redesigns.

05

Design Explorations

Testing different approaches to find the right balance.

Exploration A

Condensed Tables

Goal: Improve scanning speed for expert users

+ Fast for high-volume accounts
− Too dense for onboarding or non-expert users
Exploration B

Modular Panels

Goal: Break information into predictable blocks

+ Better grouping and clarity
− Too many modules increased scrolling
Exploration C

Simplified Rule Builder

Goal: Make complex logic easier to understand and edit

+ Cleaner flow and easier scanning
− Required new patterns for engineering to adopt

These explorations helped define the balance between clarity and density.

08

What I Learned

Key takeaways from designing systems at scale.

This project reinforced the value of designing systems, not isolated screens.

01

Predictability speeds up heavy workflows

When users know what to expect, they move faster and make fewer errors.

02

Hierarchy is essential when managing layered data

Clear visual structure helps users parse complex information quickly.

03

Reusable patterns lower engineering cost

Consistent components mean faster builds and fewer bugs.

04

Clarity always outperforms decoration

Function over form wins in enterprise environments.

05

Enterprise design succeeds when it reduces mental effort

The best interfaces get out of the way.

This work strengthened my ability to design for high-volume, data-heavy environments.
