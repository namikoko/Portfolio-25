01

Executive Summary

AI-powered financial clarity for everyday decisions.

FinSense is an AI-driven financial guidance experience designed to help people understand their spending, build healthier money habits, and make decisions with clarity instead of stress.

Most users know they should track budgets or manage spending, but the cognitive load is too high. They quit by week two.

I designed an AI-powered system that analyzes real transactions, identifies behavioral patterns, and gives users personalized recommendations in natural language. Instead of navigating charts and tabs, users simply ask questions and receive contextual, actionable answers.

Turning financial overwhelm into calm, personalized clarity.

02

The Problem

Why existing tools fail real people.

Managing money is stressful for most people, not because the tools are missing, but because the tools still expect people to behave like spreadsheets.

Three core issues shaped the opportunity:

Information Overload

Users see totals, trends, and endless categories without guidance.

Behavioral Blind Spots

People cannot easily identify what habits actually drain their budget.

Low Follow-Through

Traditional budgeting apps are rigid and time-consuming. They do not adapt to real behavior.

The problem was not numbers. It was emotional load, friction, and lack of guidance.

04

Research Insights

What users actually need from financial tools.

From interviews and spending data studies, three themes emerged:
1

People want to know what's "normal" for them

Most users don't want a perfect budget. They want to understand patterns that actually matter.

2

Behavior drives money problems, not categories

Impulse spending, unused subscriptions, inconsistent habits. Not the coffee.

3

People take action only when the recommendation feels doable

They need simple, realistic next steps, not long-term plans.

Clarity + emotional safety unlock financial follow-through.

05

Product Strategy

Reframing budgeting as guided intelligence.

The solution reframed budgeting from "track everything manually" to "let the system read your behavior and guide you."

AI Spending Advisor

Narrates trends in plain language.

Behavior Detection

Flags invisible leaks like subscriptions, impulses, and delivery spikes.

Goal-Based Prompts

Users can ask for help with budgets, savings, credit, trips, grocery lists, and more.

Chat as the Main Interface

The AI speaks like a financial coach, not a calculator.

Guidance over dashboards. Support over spreadsheets.

06

Key Design Decisions

Building for real life, not perfect discipline.

Hybrid Layout

A clean dashboard for visibility, paired with chat for deeper decisions.

Behavior-Based Insights

Instead of category totals, FinSense shows patterns like "delivery spikes" or "low-value subscriptions."

Explanations Before Solutions

The system always explains the insight first, then proposes changes.

Realistic Budget Modeling

Budgets are built around actual behavior, not idealized templates.

We designed for real life, not perfect discipline.

09

Reflection

Lessons from designing AI-powered finance.

The Problem

Budgeting tools assume users have time, structure, and discipline. They don't. People need a product that adapts to their behavior, not the other way around.

The Approach

Blend behavioral design, financial literacy, and conversational UX to reduce effort and emotional friction.

The Surprise

Users trusted the system more when it explained their patterns before offering suggestions.

The Lesson

AI is most valuable when it clarifies decision-making, not when it replaces it.

Good financial design makes users feel safe, supported, and capable.
